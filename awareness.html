Phishing Awareness Module

This short module will help you identify common phishing techniques used in deceptive emails. Please read the information carefully before continuing to the next stage.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what phishing is
  • Recognise common warning signs
  • Apply these indicators to real-world emails

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack technique where attackers impersonate legitimate organisations to trick users into revealing sensitive information or clicking malicious links.

Common Warning Signs

  • Urgency: Messages that demand immediate action
  • Generic greetings: “Dear user” instead of your name
  • Suspicious sender: Unfamiliar or misspelled domains
  • Unexpected links or attachments

Example of a Suspicious Email

From: alerts@secure-login-support.net

Subject: Action Required: Account Security Alert

Dear User,

We detected suspicious activity on your account. Please verify your identity immediately to prevent account suspension.

Verify Now

Security Team

Key Takeaway: Legitimate organisations rarely pressure users to act urgently or ask for sensitive information via email links.
